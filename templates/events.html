Position-Specific Instruction

At the high school level, taking steps to improve your game is still important. We recognize this fact and take great pride in being able to guarantee improvement for every camp attendee. Beyond skills-improvement, it’s more important than at any other stage of a football player’s development to begin focusing more on the ranking and exposure side of the game.

Toward that end, our coaches and administrators will guide and instruct athletes at this level, improving measurables scouts and recruiters are looking for when they turn their eye toward stocking up their college programs with the stars of tomorrow.

Each attendee will receive training in weight lifting techniques, conditioning and position-specific instruction to hone in those skills. Position specific events are detailed below.

Quarterbacks

- Passing Mechanics, 3-5-7 Step Drops, Sprint Out, Boot and Pass Progression, and Shotgun Footwork

The position that needs no introduction. The entire offense flows through here, where handoffs get made and touchdown passes are thrown.

Tight Ends

– Run and Pass Blocking, Pass Catching, and Route Running
